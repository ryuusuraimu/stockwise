# Primary Persona: Yuma Sato

## Background

Yuma Sato is 22 years old. He is a university student or new graduate who wants to start investing but feels overwhelmed by terms like NISA, index funds, PER, PBR, dividends, risk, and long-term investing.

He has seen investing content on social media, heard friends talk about NISA, and knows he should probably learn about money. Still, most explanations feel either too technical or too confident.

## Goals

- Understand basic investing terms without embarrassment.
- Learn what to look up first.
- Build enough confidence to keep studying.
- Compare related concepts in plain language.
- Revisit important terms later.

## Anxieties

- "What if I misunderstand an important term?"
- "What if the information is biased or trying to sell me something?"
- "What if I lose money because I trusted the wrong explanation?"
- "I do not even know what words to search for."

## Search Behavior

Yuma searches with short, mixed, and sometimes vague queries. He may use Japanese terms, English abbreviations, or natural questions.

He often starts from a term he heard somewhere, then needs help discovering the next related concept.

## Likely Search Terms

- NISA
- PER
- PBR
- dividends
- index fund
- S&P500
- Orukan
- risk
- long-term investing
- what is a stock
- beginner investing
- tax-free investing

## What Yuma Really Wants To Know

Yuma wants to know what a concept means, why it matters, what to be careful about, and what related idea he should learn next.

His deeper problem is not only lack of information. He does not know what to search for, what to trust, or what to understand first.

## How Stockwise Helps

Stockwise helps Yuma by providing clear FAQ cards, simple language, related concepts, tags, saved FAQs, and search history. It gives him a safe path through investing vocabulary without recommending stocks or implying that investing has guaranteed outcomes.
